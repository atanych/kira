# Mirroring — Deep Dive

> Repeat the last 1-3 words of what they said, with an upward inflection. Then shut up.

## What it is
The most minimal conversational move that exists. You echo 1-3 words, as a question. They keep talking. You get free information and they feel heard.

## Why it works
- **Isopraxism** — humans (and primates) reflexively mirror each other; this taps into it
- Non-threatening — you're not challenging, not agreeing, just reflecting
- Buys you thinking time without looking slow
- Triggers their urge to *finish their own thought*

## The mechanics
1. Listen for the key 1-3 words (often an emotion or a loaded phrase)
2. Repeat them **as a question** — rising tone
3. **Stop talking.** Pause. Don't ask a follow-up.

## Examples

| They say | You mirror |
|---|---|
| "We've been burned by AI tools." | "Burned?" |
| "The Dubai market has been rough this year." | "Rough this year?" |
| "We already have a CRM." | "Already have one?" |
| "Twelve hundred a month is a lot." | "A lot?" |
| "This isn't right for us." | "Right for you?" |

## Common mistakes
- **Parroting full sentences** — sounds sarcastic. Keep it to 1-3 words.
- **Flat tone** — mirroring is a *question*, not a statement. Rising inflection.
- **Filling the silence yourself** — the mirror only works if you pause after. Let them.

## When it backfires
High-tension situations where they're already escalating. Feels like mocking. Switch to Labeling (part 2).

## Volat AI application
Mirror every objection in discovery calls:
- "Уже пробовал AI" → "Уже пробовал?"
- "Слишком дорого" → "Слишком дорого?"
- "Пока думаем" → "Пока думаете?"

You will learn more in 3 mirrors than in 10 follow-up questions.

## Homework
Next normal conversation (not a call) — pick one moment, mirror 3 words, shut up. Watch it click.
