# YouTube Channel

## Role in the business
- **Year 1:** Authority + case study showcase. NOT the primary lead source.
- **Year 2+:** Inbound funnel, audience asset, consulting credibility.

## Positioning (UNDECIDED)

Two options:
1. **Broad:** "AI for business" — wider audience, slower to convert, easier content
2. **Niche:** "AI for [vertical]" — smaller audience, fast trust build, easier to sell to

**Recommendation:** start broad for 10 videos, narrow to vertical once picked. Broad videos attract the YT algorithm; niche videos convert viewers into clients.

New channel starts at zero subs. No transfer from UFC 5 audience (different topic, language intent).

## Language
Russian.

## Cadence (initial)
- 1 long-form per week (10-20 min)
- 3 shorts per week (repurposed from long)
- Livestream/Q&A when organic demand appears

## Content pillars
1. **Tutorials** — "how to build X with AI" (demos)
2. **Case studies** — "I built this for a client, here's the result"
3. **Opinion/hot takes** — "why most AI automation in RU is garbage"
4. **Tool reviews** — stay on top of what's new
5. **Business behind the scenes** — share the journey, build audience trust

## Metrics that actually matter
- **Year 1:** watch time, subscriber growth, comment quality. NOT views.
- **Year 1.5+:** inbound leads mentioning YT, case study citations.
- **Vanity:** view count, likes. Ignore.

## Integration with business
- Every productized client = potential case study video (with permission)
- Every common client question = potential video topic
- Pin a video that pitches the offer (soft CTA, not salesy)
