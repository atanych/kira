# Market

## Target: Russian-speaking entrepreneurs

Split into two tiers — same content, same language reaches both. Different pricing.

---

## Tier 1 — RU SMB (Moscow/SPB)

### Pros
- **Payment rails:** RUB direct transfer BY ИП → RU client. Smoothest rail available. No SWIFT, no conversion, 30 seconds.
- **Pricing:** $1-2k setups, $500-1k/mo support. 3-5x above BY domestic.
- **Zero trust friction** — same language, same cultural context, same time zone
- **Massive market, under-automated** vs US equivalent

### Cons
- Macro volatility — ruble tanks, SMB budgets cut fast
- Must stay Moscow/SPB tier — regional RU willingness-to-pay drops hard
- Some clients hesitant with BY providers (political minority, exists)

### Role
Volume + pipeline. Many smaller deals, faster sales cycle.

---

## Tier 2 — CIS Diaspora

### Locations
Dubai, Limassol, Tbilisi, Belgrade, Istanbul, Yerevan, Almaty, Bali. Wherever RU-speakers relocated post-2022.

### Pros
- **They pay in USD/EUR** — margin math works with fewer clients
- **Same language, zero trust friction**
- **They ARE your YT audience** — RU-speaking + AI-curious + business owners. Channel and business reinforce each other.
- **Less saturated than US market** — RU-speaking AI providers exist but it's early
- **No BY compliance problem** — serving people who also left

### Cons
- Harder to find than RU SMB — they're scattered
- Payment still needs rails (Deel/SWIFT/USDT) — not as frictionless as RUB transfer
- Sales cycle slower (bigger ticket)

### Role
Higher margin per deal. Anchor tier for hitting $15k/mo.

---

## Rejected markets

### ❌ BY domestic SMB
Willingness-to-pay is 5-10x below target. $15k/mo would need 15+ clients with huge churn. Math doesn't work.

### ❌ US/EU native market
BY location = trust hit. You'd lose to local agencies on credibility even if you're better. Payment compliance on their side flags BY beneficiaries. Not worth the uphill battle.

### ❌ Regional RU (non-Moscow/SPB)
Prices drop fast outside capital cities. Stay Moscow/SPB or diaspora.
