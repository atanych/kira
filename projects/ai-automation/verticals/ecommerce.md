# Vertical Deep-Dive: E-commerce Sellers

_Parent: [[verticals]]_

## Who exactly

### RU market
- **Wildberries sellers** — solo founders + 2-10 person teams. Russia's #1 marketplace, ~1M active sellers.
- **Ozon sellers** — similar profile, often multi-channel with WB.
- **Sweet spot:** sellers doing 5-50M RUB/year revenue, 50-500 SKUs. Big enough to need automation, small enough to not have in-house team.

### Diaspora market
- **Shopify store owners** — RU-speaking immigrants running D2C brands (supplements, fashion, beauty, home)
- **Amazon FBA sellers** — Dubai/Cyprus-based RU speakers doing private label
- **Etsy sellers** (niche but real) — handmade, print-on-demand

## Pain landscape

Ranked by intensity:

1. **Content at scale** — new SKUs need descriptions, photos edited, ad copy. Manual work eats 10+ hrs/week.
2. **Review management** — WB/Ozon reviews pile up, late responses tank rating, rating drives sales.
3. **Listing optimization** — WB's algorithm is opaque. Sellers guess what works.
4. **Customer service** — same 20 questions asked 100x/day (sizing, shipping, returns).
5. **Ad copy variation** — Yandex Direct, TG Ads, Meta need 10+ variants per campaign.
6. **Inventory/reorder decisions** — guessing demand, overstocking or running out.
7. **Abandoned cart recovery** (Shopify more than WB/Ozon)
8. **Cross-sell/upsell emails** — nobody sends them, money left on table.

## Packaged offer options

### Offer A: "AI Content Engine for Sellers"
**What:** From product feed → auto-generates descriptions, ad copy, email sequences, social posts.

- Setup: **$2,000-3,000**
- Monthly updates/support: **$300-500**
- Delivery: 10-14 days
- Margin at scale: high (same pipeline per client, different feed)
- **Best fit:** serious sellers with 100+ SKUs

### Offer B: "Review Reply Automation"
**What:** Monitors WB/Ozon reviews, generates on-brand replies, flags issues for human review.

- Setup: **$1,500**
- Monthly: **$200-300**
- Delivery: 7 days
- Margin at scale: very high (single template per client)
- **Best fit:** entry offer, easy yes

### Offer C: "Listing Optimizer"
**What:** Analyzes top-performing competitors in your category, suggests title/bullets/photo improvements.

- Setup: **$2,000** one-time OR **$400/mo** subscription
- Delivery: 14 days analysis + handoff doc
- **Best fit:** sellers who believe optimization matters

## Access path

**RU:**
- TG communities: "Клуб селлеров", "Практика на WB", "WBLife", dozens of niche groups. Very active.
- WB/Ozon conferences (Mozaico, Marketplaces Conference)
- Paid ads in seller media (Seller.ru, Marketplaces.ru)
- Cold outreach to public seller profiles on Instagram (many sellers run personal brands)

**Diaspora:**
- Amazon FBA RU-speaking TG channels (Dubai has strong community)
- Shopify RU-speaking course alumni groups
- Instagram DM to brand founders

**First-client math:** realistic to get 5-10 discovery conversations in 7 days via TG outreach.

## Unit economics

Time estimate for Offer A first delivery: **40-50 hrs**.
After SOP + tooling (client #3+): **15-20 hrs**.

Margin math (Offer A, diaspora pricing):
- Revenue: $3,000

- Infra costs (API calls, etc): ~$50/client
- Net: solid

## Risks

### Market
- **Race to the bottom** — commoditized work, many cheap RU providers undercutting
- **Tool sprawl** — WB has 50+ 3rd-party tools already, buyers have seen pitches
- **Platform dependency** — WB changes API spec → your pipeline breaks overnight
- **Hot market = fast replication** — your product gets copied in 90 days

### Client
- **Tire-kicker density** — sellers get pitched weekly, skeptical, will haggle hard
- **Churn after setup** — they don't renew monthly once initial content is generated
- **Platform rule changes** — WB bans auto-replies tomorrow, your $200/mo disappears

### Execution
- **Scope creep** — "can you also do X?" endless on e-commerce
- **Feed format variations** — every client's data is messy differently

## First 3 clients plan

Week 1:
- Identify 3 WB seller TG communities, join, observe for 2 days
- DM 20 sellers with simple pitch: "Работаю над AI-контент инструментом для WB-селлеров, могу настроить пилот за 50% стоимости с правом снять видео-кейс. Интересно?"
- Book 3-5 discovery calls

Week 2-3:
- Pilot 3 founding clients at $1,000 (from $2,000 list)
- Film case study during delivery

Week 4:
- Post first case study video, pivot to full pricing

## Decision criteria (when to commit / when to kill)

**Commit if:**
- 3+ discovery calls in week 1 without paid ads
- At least 1 client closes at $1k pilot price
- You find the delivery rhythm enjoyable

**Kill if:**
- 30 DMs → 0 discovery calls (wrong access channel or offer)
- Clients ghost after pilot delivery (case study dies)
- WB announces major API lockdown

## Kira's take

E-commerce is the safest bet on paper: biggest TAM, cleanest async fit, clearest ROI story, most accessible via TG.

**Downside:** most commoditized. You'll be one of many. Differentiation has to come from execution quality + case studies, not unique tech.

**Best offer to lead with:** Offer B (Review Reply Automation) — $1.5k, fast delivery, easy yes, opens door to Offer A upsell.
