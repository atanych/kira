# Unreasonable Hospitality

**By [[Will Guidara]]** | 2022 | 320 pages

#hospitality #leadership #culture #service #restaurant

## One-Liner

The secret to building a world-class team and business isn't perfecting your product — it's making every single person who interacts with you feel like the most important person in the room.

## Key Principles

### Hospitality is a Competitive Advantage

In any industry, not just restaurants. When your product is comparable to competitors, the experience you create is the differentiator. Guidara took [[Eleven Madison Park]] from good to #1 in the world by obsessing over how guests *felt*, not just what they ate.

### The Dreamweaver Concept

Guidara created a dedicated role whose only job was to eavesdrop on guests and create spontaneous, personalized magical moments. Heard someone mention they'd never had a NYC hot dog? A hot dog appeared on a silver platter. Hospitality as performance art.

### One-Size-Fits-One

Reject cookie-cutter service. Every interaction should feel custom-built for the person in front of you. The same gesture that delights one person might annoy another. Read the room, adapt, deliver something personal.

### Unreasonable Standards are a Gift

Holding a nearly impossible bar isn't cruel — it's how you show your team you believe they're capable of greatness. People are energized by doing extraordinary work, not comfortable mediocrity.

### Earn Informality

Start formal, build trust, then relax into genuine connection. You can't skip to casual — it comes off as sloppy. Once trust is earned, dropping formality creates real human moments.

### Hire for Warmth, Train for Skill

Look for the person who'd chase a stranger to return a dropped scarf. Technique can be taught; genuine care for other humans can't.

## Actionable Takeaways

- [ ] Create your own "Dreamweaver" role — assign someone to actively look for small, personalized gestures for clients/teammates
- [ ] Run a daily 30-minute standup focused on culture, not just tasks — celebrate wins, reinforce values
- [ ] Give ownership, not just tasks — let team members "own" specific parts of the experience
- [ ] Lead feedback with what's working — genuinely identify strengths before addressing gaps

## What You Can Skip

- **Generic Leadership Platitudes** — several chapters rehash standard business advice you've seen everywhere
- **The "Good Faith" Blind Spot** — never addresses what happens when hospitality meets entitled/abusive people
- **Early Career Backstory** — slow first chapters; the book hits its stride once EMP enters the picture

## Verdict

One killer idea — hospitality as a weapon deployable in any industry — executed beautifully through EMP stories. The [[Dreamweaver]] concept alone is worth the read. Padded with generic leadership advice that dilutes the magic. Read for the stories and mindset shift, skim the rest.
