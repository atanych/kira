# Calibrated Questions + "That's Right" — Deep Dive

> Open-ended questions starting with "how" or "what." Nothing else.

## What a calibrated question is
- Starts with **"how"** or **"what"** — no exceptions
- Open-ended (no yes/no)
- Forces the other side to think and solve *your* problem

## The Swiss Army Knife — 3 to memorize

1. **"How am I supposed to do that?"**
   → When they demand something you can't give. Forces them to justify or back off. You haven't refused, haven't caved.

2. **"What about this is important to you?"**
   → Strips surface objections. Gets to what actually matters under the ask.

3. **"How can we make this work?"**
   → The collaborative close. Reframes them from opponent to problem-solver.

## Never ask "why"
Accusatory in every language. Swap it:
- ❌ "Why did you choose that vendor?"
- ✅ "What led you to that vendor?"

Same information. Zero defensiveness.

## "That's Right" — the holy grail

| Phrase | What it means |
|---|---|
| **"You're right"** | Polite dismissal. Ending the conversation. You got nothing. |
| **"That's right"** | Deep understanding. The sale closes. The unlock. |

## How to earn "That's right"
1. Mirror (part 1) — extract
2. Label (part 2) — defuse
3. **Summarize** — combine labels + paraphrasing of their whole situation, including the emotion underneath

### Summary formula
**Volume → Pain Point → Emotional Consequence**

### Volat AI example summary
> "So let me make sure I have this right. You're bringing in decent volume — 50 leads/month — but conversion's painful, ~6%, mostly because qualification eats your time. Now with no assistant, it all lands on you, which means you're either losing leads or losing evenings. And it sounds like the fear is this gets worse before it gets better, because the season's picking up."

If accurate → "Yeah. That's right." Pause. *Then* introduce the solution.

## Common mistakes
- **Chaining questions** — ask one, wait, listen, ask the next
- **Summarizing too early** — gather first, generic summaries earn "sure, I guess"
- **Forcing "that's right"** — if they correct you, that's data. Keep listening.

## Top 5 starter calibrated questions for realtor research calls
1. How does a typical lead come into your pipeline right now?
2. What does qualification look like once a lead arrives?
3. How do you decide which leads to call back first?
4. What's the most frustrating part of your current lead flow?
5. How would your week look if qualification took half the time?

# Playbook Summary

| Technique | Purpose | Timing |
|---|---|---|
| **Mirroring** | Extract information | Any moment, low-medium tension |
| **Labeling** | Defuse emotion | High tension or emotional stakes |
| **Calibrated Q's** | Steer the conversation | After rapport, during discovery |
| **Summary** | Earn "that's right" | Before asking for commitment |

You don't need more than this. You need reps.
