# Labeling — Deep Dive

> Verbally name the emotion the other person is feeling. Don't fix it, don't validate, don't agree. Just name it.

## What it is
The power move of the Voss playbook. Naming an emotion neutralizes it — negatives defuse, positives reinforce.

## Why it works
UCLA brain scans: naming an emotion quiets the amygdala (fear/anger center) and activates the prefrontal cortex. You literally move someone from reptile brain to human brain by naming what they feel.

## The only three starters
- "It seems like…"
- "It sounds like…"
- "It looks like…"

### Never use
- ❌ "I feel…" (makes it about you)
- ❌ "I understand…" (they'll hate it)
- ❌ "I know how you feel…" (same thing)

Stay *outside* the emotion. Just point at it.

## Examples

| Situation | Label |
|---|---|
| Client angry about missed deadline | "It sounds like we've really let you down." |
| Long pause after pricing | "It seems like this is more than you expected." |
| Realtor dodging questions | "It seems like you're not sure this is worth your time." |
| Ксюшенька: "Ты опять работаешь допоздна" | "Кажется, тебе не хватает времени вместе." |
| Teammate: "Это не моя задача" | "Похоже, ощущение, что всё сваливается на тебя." |

## The Accusation Audit (pre-emptive labeling)

Before a hard ask, list every negative thing they might think about you. Out loud. In sequence.

**Example for Volat AI cold call:**
> "Vladimir, ты сейчас наверняка думаешь: 'опять какой-то AI-инструмент мне продают'. И думаешь, что я не разбираюсь в специфике зарубежки. И что это очередная американская штука, которая не работает в наших реалиях. Поэтому давай сразу — три вопроса про твой текущий лид-флоу, и если за 10 минут ничего полезного не скажу, сам меня остановишь."

**Rule:** go harder than they would. If you soft-pedal, they think you're dodging. Over-accuse yourself → they'll actually defend you. "Ну, не всё так плохо…" = unlock.

## Common mistakes
- **"I feel" / "I understand"** — deleted from your vocabulary on sales calls. Forever.
- **Labeling a feeling they don't have** — breaks trust. Hedge if unsure: "It looks like there *might* be some hesitation."
- **Labeling and continuing** — label, then shut up. The label needs silence to work.

## Volat AI application
Pre-write labels for top 3 objections:
1. **Price:** "Похоже, сумма больше, чем ты закладывал под эксперименты."
2. **Trust:** "Кажется, уже был опыт с чем-то подобным, который не сработал."
3. **Status quo:** "Похоже, текущий процесс пока справляется, и непонятно, где тут улом."

Practice out loud until it stops feeling weird. First 3 times in real calls — it will feel weird. By the 4th, it clicks.

## Homework
Next tense conversation — label one emotion out loud. Watch the temperature drop.
