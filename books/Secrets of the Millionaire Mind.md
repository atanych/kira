# Secrets of the Millionaire Mind

**Author:** [[T. Harv Eker]]
**Year:** 2005 · **Pages:** 224
**Tags:** #mindset #money #self-help #wealth

## One-Liner
Your financial outcomes are downstream of a subconscious "money blueprint" installed in childhood — change the blueprint, change the bank account.

## Key Principles

### 1. Money Blueprint
What your parents said and did about money wired your internal thermostat. Earn more than the setting allows and you'll self-sabotage back down. Step one: notice the thermostat exists.

### 2. Thoughts → Feelings → Actions → Results
You can't out-hustle a belief that money is evil or that rich people are assholes. Fix the root; the fruit follows.

### 3. Play to Win, Not "Not to Lose"
Poor people aim for comfort. Rich people aim for wealth and get comfort as a byproduct. Small goals = small outcomes.

### 4. Focus on Opportunities, Not Obstacles
Same situation, two brains: one sees risk, the other sees upside. Train yourself to ask "what could work?" before "what could go wrong?"

### 5. Pay Yourself First — Manage What You Have
Habits of allocation (invest, save, give, spend) compound harder than a raise. Start today with whatever you have.

### 6. Make Money Work for You
Trading time for dollars has a ceiling. Assets (equity, real estate, business income) don't. Build the thing that earns while you sleep.

### 7. Act in Spite of Fear
Fear doesn't disappear once you feel ready. You move anyway. Comfort zone = income zone.

### 8. Constantly Learn and Grow
Income follows capacity, and capacity follows inputs. Rich people read, study, and pay to learn.

## Actionable Takeaways
- [ ] Write down the top 3 things your parents told you about money. Decide if you still agree.
- [ ] Set up the 6-jar system: Necessities 55%, Financial Freedom 10%, Long-term Savings 10%, Education 10%, Play 10%, Give 5%.
- [ ] Raise your income target 3–5x — your current goal is likely capped by your blueprint.
- [ ] Replace "I can't afford it" with "How can I afford it?" — force the brain to solve.
- [ ] Pick one asset-building move this quarter and ship it.

## What You Can Skip
- **Touch-your-head affirmations** — seminar theater, skip the ritual.
- **Millionaire Mind Intensive upsell** — the book is the book, you don't need the seminar.
- **17 Wealth Files binary** — catchy but oversimplified.
- **Tactical investing advice** — this is a mindset book, not a strategy book. Go to [[John Bogle]] or a real advisor for the how.

## Verdict
Useful if you've never thought about how your childhood warped your relationship with money. The core idea — that your internal setpoint caps your external outcome — is real and worth the read. Skim the rituals, ignore the upsell, extract the 6-jar system plus the blueprint exercise. That's the value. Don't expect actual financial strategy — this is the mindset appetizer, not the meal.
